U.S ARMY

OUR MISSION
OUR PURPOSE REMAINS CONSTANT
To deploy, fight and win our nation’s wars by providing ready, prompt and sustained land dominance by Army forces across the full spectrum of conflict as part of the joint force.

The Army mission is vital to the Nation because we are the service capable of defeating enemy ground forces and indefinitely seizing and controlling those things an adversary prizes most its land, its resources and its population.

Army Posture Statement
The Army Posture Statement is the Secretary of the Army and the Chief of Staff of the Army's written testimony to Congress on the state of the U.S. Army. The statement describes where the Army is and what the Army has done over the last year to support the National Defense Strategy.

This unclassified summary outlines the Army’s annual accomplishments, initiatives, and priorities, based on the Army Vision and Army Strategy. It also explains the Army’s budgetary needs for maintaining its strategic priorities in the upcoming fiscal year.


Operation Sentinel Fortress: Establishing Forward Operating Base Alpha

Situation: In response to escalating regional tensions and emerging security threats, Operation Sentinel Fortress has been initiated to establish a fortified forward operating base (FOB) in strategic proximity to high-value targets. The operational environment is characterized by asymmetric threats, irregular warfare, and potential adversarial incursions.
Mission: To secure and maintain a robust operational footprint by establishing Forward Operating Base Alpha (FOB Alpha) as a forward-deployed platform for sustained military operations.

FOB Alpha will serve as a force multiplier, projecting power projection capabilities, and enhancing operational agility across the theater of operations.

Execution:
1. Infiltration and Establishment: Special Operations Forces (SOF) units will conduct clandestine insertion operations under the cover of darkness to infiltrate the designated area of operations (AO). Utilizing stealth tactics and advanced reconnaissance assets, SOF teams will secure key terrain and establish a perimeter defense.
2. Site Construction and Fortification: Engineering units will commence rapid construction and fortification efforts to erect defensive barriers, establish tactical infrastructure, and fortify critical facilities. Emphasis will be placed on force protection measures, including hardened bunkers, observation posts, and perimeter fencing to mitigate potential threats.
3. Force Integration and Readiness: Upon securing the perimeter, combat units will integrate with supporting elements to establish command and control structures, logistical support facilities, and communication networks. Continuous training and readiness drills will ensure operational proficiency and rapid response capabilities.
4. Sustainment and Resupply: Sustainment operations will be conducted to ensure the continuous flow of logistics, personnel, and materiel to FOB Alpha. Forward logistics nodes and supply chains will be established to facilitate resupply missions, medical evacuation, and equipment maintenance.
5. Intelligence and Surveillance: Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance (ISR) assets will be deployed to provide real-time situational awareness, monitor enemy movements, and gather actionable intelligence. Persistent surveillance will enable early warning and rapid response to potential threats.
